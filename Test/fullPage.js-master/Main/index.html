Bat Facts:

Did you know that there are approximately 1,1000 species of bats in the world? What's even crazier is that bats actually make up about 1/4 of the world's mammals. Sadly, despite this fact, many species of bats are going down a sad road towards extinction.

A huge cause of the declining bat population is that fact that female bats typically only birth one pup a year. This makes replenishing the bat population much harder.

Bats can be found pretty much everywhere! The only places that you won't find any are in the extreme climates such as the actics and deserts. Chances are-unless you live in one of these places- you've probably seen a bat or have some living in your backyard! Don't be scared, though, bats are pretty harmless creatures!

Bats mostly consume insects-about 70% of all species of bats consume them- however, some bats eat other things as well! Bats can also drink nectar from flowers, some eat fruit and others actually eat small animals such as frogs, lizards and even birds. For the most part, though, bats typically consume insects (mostly moquitoes). In fact, bats can consume 1,200 mosquitoes PER HOUR!

If you want to see an really amazing display of bats, take a trip to Austin, Texas where over a million bats flood the city around 8/9pm when they emerge from under South Congress Bridge to find insects to eat. Tourists from all over the country, and even world, travel to Austin to see the bat show! It should be noted that the bats only do this during the summer, so if you're planning a trip to Austin to see the bats, be sure it's during a summer month.
